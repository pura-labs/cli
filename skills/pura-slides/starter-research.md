<span class="chapter-no">I</span>

# The question we're asking

<span class="eyebrow">Motivation</span>

Why does reader engagement drop after the third scroll on long-form
content? A two-week read-depth study on pura.so traffic.

---

<span class="chapter-no">II</span>

# Method

- N = 4,200 read sessions (anon + owned)
- Instrumentation: scroll-depth + time-in-viewport
- Cohorts split by theme (paper · mono · magazine · dark)

<div class="pullquote">
  We controlled for topic type (research vs. commercial) before drawing
  conclusions about theme effects.
</div>

---

<span class="chapter-no">III</span>

# Findings

1. Magazine theme holds readers 38% longer on investigative pieces.
2. Paper theme dominates for narrative long-form; mono / dark under-
   perform on reads > 1500 words.
3. Dark theme outperforms on talks & launches — signals "event" not
   "document".

---

<span class="chapter-no">IV</span>

# Limitations

- Time-in-viewport is a proxy; actual comprehension not measured.
- The paper-theme cohort skews toward existing subscribers (selection
  bias).
- Two weeks is too short to distinguish novelty from durable preference.

---

<span class="chapter-no">V</span>

# What to try next

- A/B the default theme on /app/new for new users (no-risk).
- Instrument at the *paragraph* level — scroll-depth is the wrong unit.
- Replicate on a non-Pura traffic source to rule out audience effects.
